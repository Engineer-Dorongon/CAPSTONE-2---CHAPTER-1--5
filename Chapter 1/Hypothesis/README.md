# HYPOTHESIS OF THE STUDY

To evaluate SENSEY's comparative navigation effectiveness, the study establishes the following statistical hypotheses:

* **Null Hypothesis ($H_0$):**  
  There is **no significant difference** in the visually impaired teacher’s navigation safety, mobility, and spatial awareness when utilizing the SENSEY system compared to traditional methods (using a standard white cane).

* **Alternative Hypothesis ($H_a$):**  
  There is a **significant improvement** in the visually impaired teacher’s navigation safety, mobility, and spatial awareness when utilizing the SENSEY system compared to traditional methods (using a standard white cane).

The hypothesis of this study is not just a guess—it is a mathematically testable claim that you will prove or disprove using the empirical data gathered in **Protocol 3 (The Walking Test)**. 

During your final defense, your panel will want to see how you used your research data as concrete evidence to statistically reject the Null Hypothesis ($H_0$).

### **How the Hypothesis is Tested (Operational Specifications):**

To test this hypothesis in **Chapter 4**, you will run a **Paired t-Test** (a standard statistical formula used to compare the performance of the same user under different conditions). You will compare the data from **Trial A (White Cane Only)** against **Trial C (SENSEY with Claude AI)** across three specific metrics:

1. **Navigation Safety (Collision Counts):**  
   * *Statistical Test:* We compare the average number of physical bumps/errors in Trial A versus Trial C. 
   * *Expected Result:* SENSEY trials will show a mathematically significant reduction in collisions, rejecting $H_0$ for safety.
2. **Mobility (Completion Time in Seconds):**  
   * *Statistical Test:* We compare the average duration taken to successfully walk the classroom course in Trial A versus Trial C. 
   * *Expected Result:* SENSEY trials will show significantly faster times (or stable times with much higher safety), rejecting $H_0$ for mobility.
3. **Spatial Awareness (Comprehension Score):**  
   * *Statistical Test:* We compare the 1-to-5 layout recall scores of the user after Trial A versus Trial C. 
   * *Expected Result:* SENSEY trials with Claude AI will show significantly higher comprehension scores, proving that SENSEY provides a superior mental map of the room, rejecting $H_0$ for spatial awareness.

### **The Decision Rule (The Defense Proof):**
In Chapter 4, you will run your t-test calculation using a standard confidence level of **$\alpha = 0.05$**:
* If your calculated $p$-value is **less than $0.05$** ($p < 0.05$), you will officially **reject the Null Hypothesis ($H_0$) and accept the Alternative Hypothesis ($H_a$)**. 
* This provides your panel with mathematical, unshakeable proof that your SENSEY capstone project successfully solves the user's mobility problem!
