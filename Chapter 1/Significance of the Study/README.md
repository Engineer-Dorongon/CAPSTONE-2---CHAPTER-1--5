# SIGNIFICANCE OF THE STUDY

This study provides the rationale, timeliness, and technical justification for developing an integrated, real-time spatial navigation and classroom status monitoring tool. In an era where inclusive education and equal-opportunity employment are heavily emphasized, providing robust, high-performance workplace accommodations is vital. 

The successful implementation of SENSEY and the findings of this capstone research will directly benefit the following individuals, groups, and communities:

* **To the Visually Impaired Teachers:**  
  The primary beneficiary of this study is the **visually impaired teacher**, who will directly gain a higher level of professional independence, physical safety, and workplace confidence. By receiving automated spoken reports of student behavior and real-time guidance around floor hazards, the teacher can manage lessons and navigate their classrooms safely without relying on constant sight-guided assistants.
* **To the Students and Classroom Communities:**  
  Students will benefit from a more interactive and structured learning environment. Since the **visually impaired teacher** can independently monitor student attentiveness and respond to classroom behaviors (such as students raising hands), lesson engagement increases, creating a safer, more inclusive, and highly participative educational space.
* **To the School Administrators and Educational Institutions:**  
  This research provides school boards, SPED institutions, and educational policymakers with a practical, cost-effective model for implementing reasonable workplace accommodations. It directly supports equal-employment opportunity laws, proving that assistive technology can successfully empower professionals with visual impairments to take on active teaching roles.
* **To Future Researchers and Assistive Technology Developers:**  
  This study serves as a valuable technical reference for engineers designing wearable electronics for the visually impaired. It demonstrates how to successfully balance high-level cloud AI (like Claude-Vision) with low-latency local sensors (like stereo depth and IMU) on low-power, portable hardware.
